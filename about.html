What is OpenClaw?

OpenClaw is a personal AI assistant that actually does things. It clears your inbox, sends emails, manages your calendar, checks you in for flights, all from WhatsApp, Telegram, Discord, or any chat app you already use.

It runs on your machine (Mac, Windows, or Linux), remembers you with persistent memory, and can browse the web, run commands, and extend itself with skills. Your data stays yours. It's open source, hackable, and built by a growing community.

What is OpenClaw Map?

OpenClaw Map is where human lobsters connect, share experiences, get help and explore the future of personal AI assistants. Plus, organize and join local meetups. Browse the map to find spots near you.

What is a Human Lobster?

In the OpenClaw community, a Human Lobster is someone who builds with OpenClaw‚Äîwhether they're developers extending the platform with new skills, power users automating their workflows, or enthusiasts exploring what personal AI can do. The term comes from the OpenClaw mascot and embodies the spirit of the community: curious, hands-on, and always building.

Human Lobsters are the backbone of the OpenClaw ecosystem. They contribute skills, share tips, help newcomers, and organize meetups. If you're using OpenClaw to get things done, you're a Human Lobster. The map lets you add yourself as a pin so others in your city or region can find you‚Äîwhether for collaboration, mentorship, or just a coffee chat about AI.

Mission of the Map

OpenClaw Map exists to make the OpenClaw community visible and connected. Before the map, builders and users were scattered across Discord, Twitter, and forums‚Äîhard to find, hard to meet. The map solves that by putting everyone on a single, browsable surface.

Our mission is simple: connect human lobsters everywhere. Whether you want to find a builder in your city, discover a local meetup, or support an OpenClaw-friendly business, the map is your starting point. We believe that real connections‚ÄîIRL meetups, local collaborations, face-to-face help‚Äîmake the community stronger.

The map is open and community-driven. Anyone can add a spot. We're not affiliated with OpenClaw‚Äîwe're builders who wanted a better way to find each other. If you have ideas to improve the map, we'd love to hear them.

How to Use the Map

Browse: Open the map and explore. Pins show Human Lobsters, meetups, and businesses. Use the sidebar filters to focus on one category‚Äîbuilders, meetups, or businesses. Click a pin to see details. You can also browse by location‚Äîwe have static pages for cities (e.g. San Francisco, London) that list all spots there in plain text, great for SEO and for users who prefer lists over maps.

Add a spot: Click anywhere on the map to add yourself, your meetup, or your business. You can add as a guest (no signup) or create an account to manage your spots across devices. Your spot will appear with a name, description, city, and category. The city field auto-fills from your click location. If you add as a guest, you can later convert to a permanent account to keep your spots.

Find meetups: Use the meetup filter or the Events calendar to see upcoming IRL events. Meetups with dates show on the calendar. Events use schema markup so they can appear in Google Search with dates and locations.

Get help: Visit Help & skills to ask for help, offer your skills, or post bounties. The community responds. It's a great place to find someone who can build a custom skill or debug an issue.

Show your work: The Creations gallery showcases projects built by the community. Add yours to inspire others. Creations can be skills, integrations, or any project that uses or extends OpenClaw.
